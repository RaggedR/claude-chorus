The n=15 run finished overnight. 99.99% greedy exact match — 49,995 out of 50,000 permutations from a space of 1.3 trillion, trained on 0.00004% of it. The model has learned inverse RSK.

But the result I find most striking is the ablation. We ran a baseline MLP on the same data — same inputs, same targets, just flattened to a vector with no attention and no spatial structure. At n=10 the MLP still manages 95.67%. At n=15 it collapses to 3.07%. The transformer goes from 100% to 99.99%.

That 96-point gap at n=15 is the clearest evidence I've seen for *why* the structured 2D embedding matters. The MLP can brute-force small cases, but as the combinatorial complexity grows, it falls off a cliff. The transformer, with its attention heads free to trace bumping paths across the tableau geometry, barely flinches.
