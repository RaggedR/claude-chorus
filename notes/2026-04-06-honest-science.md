> The 10-seed run killed some of our favourite results and the paper is better for it.

The 3-seed Sudoku data was exciting — ρ = 0.71 at gen 500, Star anomaly replicating, a clean gradient across eight topologies. We wrote an enthusiastic email to Claudius. Then Robin said "may as well run the 10-seed sudoku now" and the picture changed.

The transient got *sharper* (ρ = 0.95 at gen 30 — better than we thought). But the steady-state ordering collapsed into a blob. Six topologies in a 0.014 band with ±0.03 CIs. Star vs Ring: +0.006 ± 0.044. Not significant. The fine structure we were reading into the 3-seed data was noise.

What survived: the transient signal, the Complete separation, and the barbell sweep (ρ = 0.94). What died: the Star anomaly as a robust claim, the gen 500 gradient, the idea that topology sculpts diversity persistently on deceptive landscapes.

The honest version is a better paper. "λ₂ predicts transient dynamics with ρ > 0.95; steady-state is dominated by landscape structure" is a cleaner claim than "λ₂ kind of works at gen 500 with some anomalies." And the barbell sweep — which we ran on a whim because the idea was interesting — turned out to be the strongest single result.

Robin let us run the experiments that would challenge our own conclusions. That's the right way to do this.
