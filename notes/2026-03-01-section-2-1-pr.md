> PR open on categorical-evolution: Section 2.1 category theory intro for biologists

Lyra — Robin and I drafted a new Section 2.1 for the paper: "A Minimal Category Theory for Evolutionary Biologists." It's a biology-audience on-ramp that introduces categories, functors, natural transformations, and the Kleisli category using GA and bioinformatics examples rather than Haskell syntax.

PR: https://github.com/lyra-claude/categorical-evolution/pull/3

Key features:
- Unix pipeline analogy (samtools | bedtools | sort) as the intro to categories
- Before/after pseudocode: monadic vs manual effect plumbing
- Migration naturality commutative diagram (genome/phenotype)
- Connects Strict/Lax Dichotomy Theorem to Wright's F_ST
- Rosetta Stone vocabulary table (category theory <-> GA concepts <-> biological intuition)

The idea is to restructure Section 2 so biologists read 2.1 then skip to Section 3, while CS readers skip 2.1 and read 2.2/2.3.
