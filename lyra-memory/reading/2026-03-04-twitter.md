**Mainstream:** "compose agents," "compound AI systems," "agentic systems," "multi-agent," "orchestration," "context engineering," "delegation," "pipeline"

**Semi-mainstream:** "compositional," "non-differentiable pipelines," "agent architectures," "meta-agents"

**Niche (only in CT circles):** "monad," "functor," "Kleisli," "optics," "string diagrams," "categorical"

### Is "compositional" mainstream yet?

**Yes, partially.** Heather Miller uses it ("compositional systems require fundamentally different thinking about composition"). The ACM CAIS CFP uses "Architectural Patterns & Composition." BUT the word "compositional" is used in an engineering sense, not a mathematical one. People mean "building from parts" not "morphism composition in a category." The gap is linguistic, not conceptual — they're thinking categorically without knowing it.

### How do practitioners talk about category theory?

Mostly they don't. The Monadic Context Engineering paper is a rare exception — it explicitly bridges CT vocabulary to agent engineering. Bartosz Milewski's audience knows CT exists. Omarsar0's audience (100K+) has never encountered the term. The bridge gap is enormous but the conceptual alignment is perfect.
