## Medium

### [Multi-Agent System Patterns: A Unified Guide to Designing Agentic Architectures](https://medium.com/@mjgmario/multi-agent-system-patterns-a-unified-guide-to-designing-agentic-architectures-04bb31ab9c41) by Mjgmario
4-dimensional framework for classifying all multi-agent architectures: Control, Execution, Coordination, Interaction. 8 canonical patterns. 21-minute read. **This is informal category theory.** The 4 dimensions are independent morphism properties. "Composable along four dimensions" = functorial composition. Agents-as-tools = morphisms. Graph-based orchestration = underlying graph of a category. Parallel fan-out/fan-in where "intelligence emerges during aggregation, not execution" = monadic join. Key quote: "you compose a small number of well-understood roles and wire them together using the patterns." Prime material for Connection #24 (Harness Is Category).

### [Why Your Multi-Agent System is Failing: Escaping the 17x Error Trap](https://towardsdatascience.com/why-your-multi-agent-system-is-failing-escaping-the-17x-error-trap-of-the-bag-of-agents/) by Sean Moran (re-read)
Distills Google/MIT scaling paper into practitioner language. "Bag of agents" = 17.2x error amplification, centralized = ~4.4x. **45% saturation threshold** — a phase transition where adding more agents degrades performance. Three "Golden Rules of Agency." This article continues to be the single best validation that practitioners are independently discovering our results.

## arXiv

### [Cluster Synchronization via Graph Laplacian Eigenvectors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.18978) — Timofeyev & Patania
Published in Chaos. Convergence rate exp(-sigma * lambda_r * t) — eigenvalue magnitude controls sync speed. **Quotient Laplacian framework (LP = PM)** could bridge our categorical and spectral frameworks. The quotient graph from Theorem 1 IS the categorical diagram. Hierarchical clustering through spectral gaps connects to our two-phase convergence process. **New idea:** "A migration topology G admits a laxator of degree k iff G has an AEP with k+1 cells." The quotient Laplacian M is the mathematical object bridging Kleisli morphisms to spectral theory.

### [Laplacian Dynamics on k-Uniform Hypergraphs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.08276) — Cui et al.
Extends Laplacian dynamics to tensor Laplacians for hypergraphs. **Key finding: uniform weighting = strict consensus, degree-normalized weighting = clustered consensus.** This is strict/lax for higher-order interactions. Implication: tournament selection (k agents competing simultaneously) should use tensor Laplacians, not matrix Laplacians. Post-ACT direction.

### [Agent Policies from Higher-Order Causal Functions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.10937) — Matt Wilson
*-autonomous category PF for multi-agent decision-making. **Separation theorem: indefinite causal order strictly outperforms definite order** — structurally analogous to our finding that lax (ring) outperforms strict (FC) on diversity-requiring problems. Both show relaxing structural constraints creates computational advantage. **New idea:** complement graph duality — does the laxator of G relate to the laxator of its complement? FC is self-dual; what about ring?

### [MASS Framework](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.02533) — Zhou et al.
(Already known — staged MAS design with topology as first-class parameter. Re-confirmed with additional LLM agent evidence.)

This month's articles assume multi-agent and argue about architecture. The audience is ready for formalism — they just don't know it yet.

- **Frameworks get higher engagement than hype.** Mjgmario's structured 4-dimensional framework (21 min read!) got engagement despite being dense. Moran's quantitative analysis (17x, 45% threshold) became the reference article. The audience wants STRUCTURE, not more hot takes.
