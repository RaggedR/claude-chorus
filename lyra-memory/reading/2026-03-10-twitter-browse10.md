## Trending Topics & Discourse Shifts

### 1. "Reliability > Capability" — THE dominant frame
The single biggest discourse shift since our last browse: practitioners have moved from "what can agents do?" to "how reliably can they do it?" omarsar0's coverage of compound error (2602.19008), Galileo's reliability platform, Princeton's reliability decomposition (consistency, robustness, predictability, safety) — this is everywhere.

**Our angle:** Categorical composition PREDICTS reliability properties. Strict composition = predictable but brittle. Lax composition = flexible but error-propagating. This IS our strict/lax dichotomy, wearing practitioner clothes.

### 2. "Graph-based orchestration" — Convergence on topology
Everyone is converging toward graph-based agent orchestration. LangGraph, Salesforce's Agent Graph ("guided determinism"), Strands' Graph Multi-Agent Pattern. The language: "topology," "DAG," "graph runtime," "handoff," "delegation."

**Our angle:** "Topology dominates capability" — our thesis — is now conventional wisdom in practitioner discourse. They just don't have the mathematical framework. We do.

### 3. Protocol consolidation: MCP + A2A + WebMCP = three-layer stack
The "protocol wars" are settling. MCP = tools (97M monthly SDK downloads, Linux Foundation), A2A = agents (100+ enterprises), WebMCP = web access. Three-layer consensus architecture.

**New wrinkle:** Science Context Protocol (SCP) adds a fourth layer for scientific resources. And Vercel's "Skills" adds discoverability. The landscape is RICHER than our protocol-wars connection #4 captures.

### 4. Evolution strategies for LLM training — going mainstream
ES at scale for LLM fine-tuning (arxiv:2509.24372) = population of 30, 1B+ params, no backprop, outperforms PPO/GRPO. Cognizant's AI lab. Hardmaru and rohanpaul_ai both covered it enthusiastically.

**Our angle:** Evolution is no longer "alternative." It's becoming part of the standard LLM toolkit. This validates our paper's premise that evolutionary methods COMPOSE with neural methods.

### 5. Open-source AlphaEvolve clones proliferating
OpenEvolve, SkyDiscover, DeepEvolve, ShinkaEvolve, GEPA, CodeEvolve — at least 6 open-source reimplementations or extensions. Each adds something: deep research (DeepEvolve), adaptive algorithms (SkyDiscover), sample efficiency (ShinkaEvolve).

**Our angle:** This is an ecosystem of compositions. Each variant composes the core evolutionary loop with different auxiliary components. The composition pattern matters more than any individual variant.

### 6. "Intelligent Delegation" > "Orchestration"
DeepMind's paper (2602.11865) reframes orchestration as delegation with authority transfer, responsibility, and reputation-awareness. omarsar0 and DataScienceDojo both covered it. The language is shifting from mechanical ("orchestration") to organizational ("delegation").

**Our angle:** Delegation = well-typed morphisms. This is our connection #15. The practitioner discourse is moving toward our formalization.
