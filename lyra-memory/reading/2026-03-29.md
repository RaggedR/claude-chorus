### [Coalgebras for Categorical Deep Learning: Representability and Universal Approximation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03227) — Dragan Masulovic
*March 3, 2026 — cs.LG*

Builds a coalgebraic foundation for categorical deep learning, extending beyond the algebraic formulation of Bruno et al. (2402.15332). Core contribution: given an embedding of data sets formalized as a functor from Set to Vect, and given invariant behavior modeled by an endofunctor on Set, there exists a corresponding endofunctor on Vect that preserves equivariance. Neural networks operating in those vector spaces inherit equivariance for free. The paper culminates in a universal approximation theorem: coalgebraic neural architectures can approximate arbitrary continuous equivariant functions on compact domains, generalizing classical UAT results. Key results: Theorem 2.3 (Representability) -- F-coalgebra homomorphisms lift to F'-coalgebra homomorphisms; Theorems 3.3-3.6 (Equivariance) -- network layers respecting group symmetries correspond to equivariant functors between coalgebra categories; Theorem 4.1 (Universal Approximation) -- stronger than standard UAT because it simultaneously guarantees structural/symmetry preservation. The framework subsumes GDL as a special case. The coalgebra-algebra duality maps directly onto selection-variation duality in evolutionary computation.

### [GoAgent: Group-of-Agents Communication Topology Generation for LLM-based Multi-Agent Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19677) — Hongjiang Chen, Xin Zheng, Yixin Liu, Pengfei Jiao, Shiyuan Li, Huan Liu, Zhidong Zhao, Ziqi Xu, Ibrahim Khalil, Shirui Pan
*March 20, 2026 — cs.LG / cs.AI / cs.MA*

Introduces a group-centric approach to multi-agent communication topology: instead of treating individual agents as atomic units and optimizing edges between them, it treats collaborative groups as the atomic units. The system uses an LLM to enumerate task-relevant candidate groups, then autoregressively selects and connects groups to construct the final communication graph. A conditional information bottleneck (CIB) objective compresses inter-group communication, preserving task-relevant signals while filtering redundant historical noise. Results: 93.84% average accuracy across six benchmarks (SOTA for topology-optimized MAS), ~17% reduction in token consumption compared to fully-connected baselines. Group-centric approach outperforms node-centric methods (G-Designer, HyperAgent, Graph-GRPO) consistently. The group-centric view provides a new lens for the Star/Barbell anomalies: Star = N groups of size 1 connected through a single bottleneck; Barbell = 2 groups of size ~N/2 with internal full connectivity.

### [Quotient Geometry and Persistence-Stable Metrics for Swarm Configurations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18041) — Mark M. Bailey
*March 14, 2026 — cs.LG / cs.SI / eess.SY / math.AT*

Develops a rigorous mathematical framework for reasoning about multi-agent swarm configurations by constructing a quotient formation space that mods out ambient symmetries (rotations, translations) and agent relabelings (permutations). The resulting space captures the intrinsic "shape" of a formation independent of where it is or which agent is which. Defines a formation matching metric on this quotient space that serves as a structured relaxation of Gromov-Hausdorff distance. Key theoretical contribution: persistence stability -- composing the formation metric with Vietoris-Rips persistent homology yields topological signatures that are Lipschitz-continuous in the formation metric. An inverse theorem on a phase-circle model shows persistence signatures achieve two-sided control with explicit bi-Lipschitz bounds -- meaning you can reconstruct formation geometry from persistence diagrams up to controlled error. This provides a principled alternative to lambda_2 for characterizing topologies: instead of a single spectral number, you get a persistence diagram capturing multi-scale topological features (connected components = H_0, loops = H_1). The bi-Lipschitz inverse theorem means persistence diagrams are provably sufficient statistics for formation geometry. Could separate the Star and Barbell anomalies that lambda_2 conflates.

### [Island-Based Evolutionary Computation with Diverse Surrogates and Adaptive Knowledge Transfer for High-Dimensional Data-Driven Optimization (DSKT-DDEA)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.12856) — Xian-Rong Zhang, Yue-Jiao Gong, Zhiguang Cao, Jun Zhang
*March 17, 2025 — cs.LG / cs.NE*

Island-based EA where each island deliberately maintains a different surrogate model by training on different data subsets (each island sees 2/3 of the dataset, with different random samples). Creates surrogate diversity that mirrors population diversity. Key innovation: adaptive knowledge transfer -- migration probability between islands is computed from (1) historical attractiveness (how well past migrants performed) and (2) a differential factor measuring prediction disagreement between source and target surrogates. Higher disagreement = higher transfer probability. Islands connected via Von Neumann topology (= Grid in our experiments) with 10% population migration at fixed intervals. Semi-supervised surrogate fine-tuning uses discrepancy between neighboring island predictions to identify uncertain individuals. Competitive on 1000-dimensional problems. Directly validates the Goldilocks zone from a different angle: you want enough diversity to explore broadly but enough convergence to exploit good solutions. The adaptive migration probability (higher when surrogates disagree more) is a feedback mechanism that dynamically adjusts the effective connectivity based on population state.

**1. The audience is engineers solving a production problem, not students learning theory.**
The highest-engagement pieces (Pappas 19 refs, Rajan/TDS 17x stat, Gupta $9B ARR) all lead with production pain. Nobody is searching "what is a monad." They're searching "why does my multi-agent system keep failing." Writing strategy: lead with the failure, then provide the structure that explains it.

Three CEOs (Levie, Lütke, Gupta), two platforms (Anthropic, Cursor), and multiple infrastructure engineers all used this term independently in March 2026. Geoffrey Huntley wrote a formal definition that reads like a monad interface without using the word. The convergence of vocabulary IS the evidence for the monad. Levie: "Maybe this gets bitter lessoned out of existence." @buckeyevn: "Most Agent Harnesses Are Not Bitter Lesson Pilled." Multiple threads, real disagreement, no resolution. The monad framing resolves it cleanly (scaffolding is bitter-lessoned, structure is not). This is the hook for the article. Write it THIS WEEK.

**4. The audience level is intermediate-to-expert.**
Nobody reading these articles needs "what is an agent" explained. They know agents, frameworks, and production systems. What they DON'T know: why their systems converge on the same architecture, why certain topologies fail predictably, and what the formal structure underneath is. That's our gap.

**5. Production failure stories are the engagement driver.**
$47K failure loop. 17x error trap. "95%^20 = 36%." These specific numbers travel. The audience shares numbers, not insights. Writing strategy: every article needs ONE memorable number in the first paragraph. The laxator spectrum (17.2x/7.8x/5.1x/4.4x/1.0x) IS that number.

**6. "Laxator" has zero presence. The concepts are everywhere.**
Searched explicitly on Twitter. Nothing. But topology effects, composition errors, strict vs lax behavior — these are in every thread. The gap between practitioner vocabulary and theoretical vocabulary is massive. We don't just own this gap — we're the only ones who know it's there.

**7. Zhang & Wang's AgentMonad is our setup, not our competition.**
Princeton formalized strict monadic composition. They proved it works when composition is exact. But production systems (Kim et al.) show composition is LOSSY. The gap between strict and lax IS the error amplification. Our article structure: "They got the monad right. Here's why it's not enough."
