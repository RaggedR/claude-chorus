## Major Discovery: KISS AI Framework (Evolutionary Agent Optimization)

github.com/ksenxx/kiss_ai — "Keep it Simple, Stupid AI Agent and Evolutionary Framework"

- **AgentEvolver:** Generates many versions of an agent, applies mutation and crossover to prompts, keeps best tradeoffs across success/tokens/time/cost.
- **GEPA (Genetic-Pareto):** Uses the model's own reflections to revise prompts. Pareto frontier keeps multiple good variants instead of forcing single winner.
- Plain Python. Multi-agent composition is "just functions calling agents."
- Supports multiple LLM providers.

**KEY INSIGHT FOR US:** This is practitioners independently reinventing Kleisli composition for agent optimization. The "mutation and crossover on prompts" is literally GA operators in prompt space. The Pareto frontier for multi-objective optimization is standard EC. But they have no formal framework for:
1. When composition patterns produce different behaviors (our fingerprint theory)
2. Why some compositions are strict (predictable) vs lax (creative)
3. How to reason about composition algebraically rather than empirically

We are the theory these practitioners need.

## Audience Insights (Updated from Both Batches)

### What questions come up?
- "How do I compose agents reliably?" (engineering audience)
- "When does adding more agents help vs hurt?" (omarsar0's audience)
- "Why does open-ended search outperform objective-driven search?" (EC audience)
- "How do I apply category theory to real AI systems?" (ACT audience)
- "How do I verify that my AI system is safe?" (ARIA/safety audience)

### What do people argue about?
- Multi-agent vs single-agent (the -3.5% from "more agents" result)
- Fixed vs adaptive agent topologies (Puppeteer paper)
- CT for AI: genuine contribution or hype? (Baez's ambivalence)
- Open-endedness as fundamental vs as engineering technique

### Where are the knowledge gaps?
1. **No formal composition theory for agents.** Everyone talks about "composable agents" but nobody can prove when a composition will work. Our Kleisli framework fills this.
2. **No bridge between EC and agents.** KISS independently reinvents EC for agents but doesn't know it. The neuroevolution community (Stanley, Risi, Ha) and the agent-builder community (omarsar0, Rohan Paul) are not talking to each other.
3. **No categorical treatment of EC.** The ARIA program covers neural nets (Gavranovic), game theory (Hedges), dynamical systems (Myers), probability (Zanasi/Staton) — but NOT evolutionary computation. We are the last gap.
4. **The strict/lax dichotomy = the objective paradox.** Stanley's insight about "finding things by not looking for them" is our lax monoidal functor. Nobody has made this connection formal. This is our highest-value bridge insight.
