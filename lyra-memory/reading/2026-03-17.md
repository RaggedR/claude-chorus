## Web Research / arXiv

### HIGH PRIORITY — Must-Cite

**Li et al. — "A Categorical Framework for Quantifying Emergent Effects in Network Topology" (arXiv:2311.17403, revised Jan 2025)**
Formalizes emergence as "structural nonlinearity" using homological algebra and cohomology. This is the SAME phenomenon as the laxator from a completely different mathematical tradition. Hedges: "emergence = failure of lax functor to be strict"; Li et al.: derived functors; us: laxator tensor. **Three formalisms, same concept. MUST-CITE for ACT.**

**Wu & Brandes — "Threshold Graphs are Globally Synchronizing" (arXiv:2511.12646, revised Feb 2026)**
Proves threshold graphs achieve global synchronization in Kuramoto — ALL trajectories converge. FC is a threshold graph. Graph-theoretic proof for WHY FC is maximally strict. Degree sequence majorization parallels algebraic connectivity ordering. **MUST-CITE for ACT.**

**Ostilli — "Topologically Protected Synchronization in Networks" (arXiv:2503.18272, revised Oct 2025)**
Nodes with identical neighbor sets synchronize readily; this sync is UNALTERED by changes in the rest of the graph. "Independent pacemakers." = local structure determines sync robustness. **STRONG-CITE.**

### MEDIUM PRIORITY — GECCO / Background

**Small-World Networks for Multi-Agent Intelligence (arXiv:2512.18094, Dec 2025)** — Applies SW networks to LLM multi-agent systems. Uses {ring, star, FC, random} as baselines. Uncertainty-guided rewiring = dynamic version of random topology. GECCO citation.

**OFA-MAS (arXiv:2601.12996, Jan 2026, WWW 2026)** — Universal topology generator for multi-agent systems. Trying to learn the functor from tasks to topologies.

**Hays — "Hebbian-Oscillatory Co-Learning" (arXiv:2603.08731, Feb 2026)** — Kuramoto phase-locking replacing attention. Shows Kuramoto dynamics crossing from physics into ML engineering.
