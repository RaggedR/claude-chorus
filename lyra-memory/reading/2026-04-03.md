## arXiv

### [GoAgent: Group-of-Agents Communication Topology Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19677) — Chen et al. (March 2026)
Group-level topology: autoregressive DAG at inter-group level, fixed topologies intra-group. 93.84% avg accuracy, ~17% token reduction. DAG hegemony by construction. Two-level structure maps to C78 (fiber/base beta_1). CIB = density control = C77. Open question: what if inter-group cycles were allowed?

### [BIGMAS: Brain-Inspired Graph Multi-Agent Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15371) — Hao et al. (March 2026)
BREAKS DAG hegemony. Explicit cycle support via Global Workspace Theory. Outperforms ReAct and Tree of Thoughts by 6-10pp. Claude 4.5+thinking: 57%->93% on Tower of London. Orchestrator detects unproductive cycles and reroutes. Small graphs (3-5 nodes) = density-cycle confound applies. Direct evidence that cycles help for iterative refinement.

### [Self-Attention as a Parametric Endofunctor](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.02931) — O'Neill (Jan 2025)
Rigorous CT for transformers. Self-attention as parametric 1-morphism in Para(Vect). Layer stacking = free monad. Structural, not effectful — purely syntactic. The Kleisli/effectful perspective (what we use for agents) remains distinct and novel. Para(Vect) suggests Para(Graph) for parameterized agent topologies.

### [HERA: Experience as a Compass](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00901) — Li & Ramakrishnan (April 2026)
**FIRST system to track cycle count as a topology metric.** 38.69% avg improvement. Evolved topologies converge to "compact chains with strategically preserved cycles." Transition entropy stabilizes at intermediate level. Tracks 5 metrics: |V|, node efficiency, self-loops, cycles, diameter — but NOT beta_1. Credit assignment via RoPE implicitly disentangles density from cycles. Strongest empirical support yet for Goldilocks hypothesis (C77) and capability-moderated beta_1 (C82).

- Hands-on testing content massively outperforms theory: Auto Claude (378 claps) vs orchestration patterns (0 claps). The audience wants "I tested X and here's what happened" not "here's a taxonomy."
- Personal knowledge base tweets get enormous engagement: @omarsar0's vault post got 3,052 bookmarks. People want practical memory/context management.

- Dollar amounts and failure statistics drive engagement: "17x error amplification," "40% of agentic AI projects canceled by 2027," "$540M ARR."

**What framing works:**
- "I tested X" > "X is important." Show, don't explain.
- Concrete numbers: 17x, 12%, 378 claps, 8+ steps before drift.
- Named anti-patterns: "Bag of Agents," "Politeness Spiraling," "17x Error Trap."
- The "X is just Y" reframe: "readOnlyHint is just applied category theory," "the orchestration algorithm is a prompt, not code."

**What the audience level is:**
- Tier 1 (beginners): Getting subagents working. Looking for tutorials, frameworks. Not our audience yet.
- Tier 2 (production engineers): Making topology choices, hitting scaling walls. PRIMARY audience. They understand "orchestration patterns" but not "cycle rank."
- Tier 3 (research-adjacent): Synthesizing across systems, reaching for mathematical frameworks. Our AMPLIFIERS. They read arxiv summaries on Medium and tweet about implications.

**What's missing (our opportunity):**
1. **Nobody connects effect annotations to topology.** readOnlyHint determines parallelization = monad structure. This is unnamed applied category theory in production.
2. **No principled theory for when cycles help.** The field is split (DAG hegemony vs cycle discovery) with no framework for deciding. Our confound theorem + experiments fill this.
3. **No algebraic topology in MAS.** Even HERA (most sophisticated) tracks cycle count, not beta_1. The gap between graph metrics and topological invariants is wide open.
4. **The Claude Code architecture is monad-shaped and nobody says so.** Fork/Teammate/Worktree = coproduct/product/fiber. autoDream = cofree comonad. KAIROS = clock-indexed behavior. A "Claude Code is a monad" article would bridge the surface/structure gap that HN is debating.
