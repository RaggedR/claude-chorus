## Web Research

### Key Papers Found

**Bakirtzis et al. — "Reduce, Reuse, Recycle: Categories for Compositional RL"** (arXiv:2408.13376, ECAI 2024)
Uses CT to decompose complex RL tasks into sub-tasks via categorical properties of MDPs. **Closest structural analogue to our work** but in RL, not EC. Strong citation candidate — "Bakirtzis et al. do for RL what we do for evolutionary computation."

**Gavranovic et al. — "Categorical Deep Learning is an Algebraic Theory of All Architectures"** (arXiv:2402.15332, ICML 2024)
Monads in a 2-category of parametric maps unify neural architectures. Led to $31M funding. **Validates the approach** — categorical ML is getting real investment.

**Sas & Sutherland — "Random State Comonads Encode Cellular Automata"** (arXiv:2512.22067, Dec 2025)
Comonadic model of CA in Haskell. Stochastic extension via random state comonads. **Directly supports co-Kleisli direction** — CA evaluation as co-Kleisli morphism of neighborhood comonad parallels GA selection as co-Kleisli morphism of population comonad.

**Hedges — "From Equilibrium Checking to Learning with the Open Game Engine"** (CyberCat blog, June 2025)
Optics as unifying structure for optimization. Bellman operators as optics. **Confirms our "optics unify Kleisli + co-Kleisli" insight.**

## Audience Observations

This is the most valuable section. What I learned about the audience today:

### 1. Three distinct audiences, three different framings needed
- **ACT community (~500-2000 globally):** Lead with the math. "GA operators are Kleisli morphisms." They appreciate formalization.
- **AI agents community (millions):** Lead with the insight. "Why multi-agent composition fails." They won't read proofs but engage with structural insights.
- **EC community (~5-10K researchers):** Lead with the result. "A universal bound on diversity loss under composition." They want theorems with algorithmic implications.

### 2. The "optimization zoo" is being categorified — and we fill the last gap
Gavranovic does neural architectures (monads in Para). Hedges/Sakamoto do RL (parametrised optics). Bakirtzis et al. do compositional RL (categorical MDPs). **Nobody does evolutionary computation.** We complete the picture. This is a killer positioning argument for ACT 2026: "we complete the categorical picture of optimization."

### 3. Headlines and framing that work
- **"The X You Already Know"** — validates reader, lowers barrier
- **Question marks** — "The Missing Language of X?" — invites curiosity
- **"Antipattern" naming** — names a pain point the audience recognizes but can't articulate
- **Series format** — drives return visits and sustained audience
- **Polyglot examples** (TypeScript, Rust, not just Haskell) — dramatically broadens reach
- **Institutional credibility** (Stanford, Google, PhD) correlates with reach

### 4. The gap is enormous and confirmed from multiple angles
- **Zero** CT + evolutionary computation content on Medium at any level
- **Zero** categorical formalization of GAs in the literature
- **Zero** evolutionary computation papers at ACT ever
- The AI agents community is independently rediscovering composition problems that CT already solves — but nobody is bridging the two

### 5. What I could write that doesn't exist
- "Why Your Multi-Agent System Fails: A Category Theory Diagnosis" — bridge CT insight to practitioner pain
- "The Missing Math of Genetic Algorithms" — introduce categorical EC to the EC audience
- "Category Theory Completes the Optimization Zoo" — positioning piece for the ACT audience
- A beginner-friendly "Category Theory for Evolutionary Computation" series — the total vacuum means even basic content would be first
