## arXiv

### [Decorated Cospans at Work](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12302) — Wesley Phoa
Demonstrates decorated cospans for composing heterogeneous dynamical systems along shared interfaces. The decoration functor is explicitly a **lax monoidal functor** (citing Fong's framework) -- the same structure we use for island-model GA formalization. Composition via pushouts along variable identifications is structurally analogous to our migration topology: "does not care what is inside the models; it cares only about what they share." When coupling is zero, composition degenerates to independent product (strict monoidal case); non-trivial coupling introduces laxity -- mirrors our none-topology (strict) vs ring/star/FC (lax). The bias decomposition (sampling, structural, observational) sparks an idea: could we decompose our laxator analogously? Structural bias maps to topology-induced laxator; observational maps to fitness-landscape effects. This could explain why W = 1.0 (structural dominates observational).

### [Composable Uncertainty in Symmetric Monoidal Categories](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09430) — Furter, Huang, Zardini (ACT 2025 proceedings)
Integrates uncertainty into open systems via change-of-base on enriched categories. Symmetric monoidal monads generate Markov categories. The deterministic (strict) case embeds into the uncertain (lax) case via a **strict symmetric monoidal functor** -- precisely our strict/lax dichotomy. Composition via monad strength is structurally identical to how Kleisli composition threads stochasticity through GA operators. Key spark: parametrize our framework by monads T_G indexed by graph G, where monad strength proportional to lambda_2(G). The strict embedding becomes T_disconnected (no coupling), and our laxator phi_G is literally the deviation from strictness. The 2-categorical structure (reparametrization 2-cells) might formalize why different fitness landscapes produce equivalent diversity orderings.

### [A Simple Categorical Calculus of Interacting Processes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18321) — Nester, Voorneveld
Process interaction modeled via multicategories. Starting with non-interactive processes (morphisms of M), interaction is obtained through categorical structure (the free cornering). Proven confluent and terminating with deadlock freedom. Virtual double categories (weaker than double categories) match our situation: not all topology compositions are well-defined. Key spark: deadlock freedom guarantees convergence, but the choice of multicategory (= topology) determines WHERE it converges. This reframes our spectral bridge: lambda_2 governs not just convergence rate (known) but convergence TARGET (our novel contribution).

### [Persistent Homology for Population Diversity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.14496) — Kii, Yaji, Teramoto, Fujita
Replaces crowding distance in NSGA-II with Wasserstein distance between persistence diagrams. Captures structural diversity that L^p norms miss. Dual perspective to our work: we use topology (communication graph) as control mechanism; they use topology (persistent homology) as measurement tool. Key spark: apply PH to our experimental populations directly. Compute persistence diagrams for each topology. Prediction: W_p(PD_none, PD_ring) < W_p(PD_none, PD_FC) because ring preserves more topological features. This would give our strict/lax ordering a TDA-based characterization independent of lambda_2.

### [Evolutionary Policy Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.19037) — Wang, Su, Gupta, Pathak
EPO merges EA operators (crossover, mutation, selection) with policy gradients for RL. Maintains K agents sharing a single actor-critic but conditioned on unique latent embeddings. Flat population, no communication topology -- this is the FC extreme in our framework. The gating condition (operators activate only when fitness spread exceeds threshold) is a strict-to-lax switch: "strict" when meaningful differentiation exists, "lax" (operators suppressed) when not. Key spark: could latent embeddings be understood as "soft topology"? If behavioral distances between embeddings define an implicit graph, does its spectral property predict diversity?

### [AI-Driven Consensus with Path-Laplacian Matrices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.06894) — Ahsini, Reverte, Conejero
Extends consensus dynamics from 1-hop to k-hop interactions using path-Laplacian matrices L_k. Multi-hop model: dx/dt = -sum alpha_k L_k x(t) with exponential decay alpha_k = e^(-alpha*k). At alpha=0, all hops equally weighted (approaching FC). At alpha -> infinity, only 1-hop matters (prescribed topology). **Key spark:** The decay parameter alpha defines a continuous interpolation between topologies -- a continuous version of our strict-lax spectrum. Our laxator phi_G might be related to optimal alpha for graph G: phi_G = f(alpha*(G)). The path-Laplacian also explains why ring and star have similar behavior at small n: 2-hop neighbors in a 5-ring ARE the star's connections.

### [Population-Based Neural Combinatorial Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08696) — Irazusta Garmendia, Ceberio, Mendiburu
Taxonomy of population awareness levels: Level 1 (independent = our "none"), Level 2 (contextual = ring/star), Level 3 (joint = FC). Introduces exploration weight omega in [0,1] that continuously interpolates between exploitation (omega=0, strict) and exploration (omega=1, lax). The omega parameter IS our laxator phi_G made explicit and tunable. The k-NN memory is emergent topology: k=1 is sparse/ring-like, k=all is FC. Key question: if omega is learned rather than scheduled, does the converged omega* correlate with lambda_2 of the implicit topology?

### [Persistent Homology for Population Diversity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.14496) — Kii et al. (duplicate -- read in both CT and TDA context)
See above. The dual nature of "topology" (communication graph vs. TDA of solutions) is itself a connection worth formalizing.
