## Deep Reads

### [Comonads — Bartosz Milewski](https://bartoszmilewski.com/2017/01/02/comonads/)
The clearest explanation of the monad/comonad duality I've found. Key insight: **a comonad is a container that comes pre-filled with contents**. You can peek at it (`extract`) but you can't put things in. A co-Kleisli arrow `w a → b` takes a value *with its whole context* and produces a result. This is "contextual computation."

Three concrete comonads that matter for us:
- **Store comonad**: `(s → a, s)` — a function from an index to values, plus a current focus. This is what lenses are. For GAs: could model a population as a Store where `s` indexes individuals and `a` is their data.
- **Stream comonad**: infinite stream with a focus position. `extend` applies a function at every position using the whole stream as context. Co-Kleisli arrows are digital filters. For GAs: could model generation-over-time as a stream, where operators look at history.
- **Env comonad**: `(e, a)` — a value tagged with an environment. Dual to Reader. Simplest model for "an individual with its population context."

**Relevance to GAs**: Selection is inherently a co-Kleisli arrow — it takes an individual *in the context of the whole population* and produces a result. The question is which comonad captures "population context" best: `Env Population`, `Store PopIndex`, or something custom?

### [Dynamical Systems and Their Steady States — n-Category Cafe](https://golem.ph.utexas.edu/category/2018/04/dynamical_systems_and_their_st.html)
The Baez-Pollard framework and Myers' work on open dynamical systems. The key theorem: **black-boxing is a functor**. This means you can study steady states (and trajectories, and periodic orbits) of a composed system by:
1. Studying the steady states of each component
2. Composing the results

The composition of steady-state behaviors respects the composition of systems. This is exactly the structure we need for diversity fingerprints: if the fingerprint (a behavioral invariant) is functorial, then the fingerprint of a composed strategy is determined by the fingerprints of its components.

Myers' later work: "all covariantly representable structures of dynamical systems — including trajectories, steady states, and periodic orbits — compose according to the laws of matrix arithmetic."

### [Uustalu & Vene — Comonadic Notions of Computation](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1571066108003435) (via search summaries)
The theoretical foundation for comonads-as-context. Their thesis: comonads structure **context-dependent computation** in the same way monads structure effectful computation. Instances:
- Dataflow (stream-based computation)
- Attribute evaluation (tree relabelling)
- Cellular automata

All these have the same pattern: each computation step depends on a surrounding context (neighbors, ancestors, history). GA selection fits this pattern — each selection depends on the whole population.

They also discuss **distributive laws of a comonad over a monad** — combining context-dependent and effectful computation. For GAs, this would model operators that are both context-dependent (selection) AND effectful (random mutation). This is the full picture.

## Connections

### 1. Diversity Fingerprints Are Black-Boxed Functors
The Baez/Myers framework proves that behavioral invariants of dynamical systems (steady states, trajectories) compose functorially. Our diversity fingerprints claim that qualitative diversity trajectories are determined by composition structure. **These are the same claim.** If we can frame GAs as open dynamical systems and diversity fingerprints as a "black-boxing" operation, we get a theorem for free: the fingerprint of a composed strategy is a function of the component fingerprints.

This elevates diversity fingerprints from "empirical observation" to "consequence of a general theorem about compositional dynamical systems." That's a much stronger paper.

### 2. The Right Comonad for GAs
From the three comonads I read about today:
- `Env Population Individual` — simplest. An individual tagged with its population. Selection is `Env Pop Ind → Ind`.
- `Store PopIndex Individual` — richer. Population as an indexed store. Can model "look at neighbor" operations (like spatial GAs).
- Custom comonad combining population context with generation history — most expressive but hardest to formalize.

Starting with `Env` is the right move. It's simple enough to prove things about and captures the core idea (context-dependence on population).

### 3. Distributive Laws = Full GA Model
Uustalu & Vene's distributive laws of comonads over monads model computation that is BOTH context-dependent AND effectful. A GA pipeline is exactly this: selection depends on population context (comonadic), mutation introduces randomness (monadic). A distributive law `W (M a) → M (W a)` would formalize how these interact. This could be the theoretical core of the co-Kleisli paper.
