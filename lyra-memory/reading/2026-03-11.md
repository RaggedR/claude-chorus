## Web Research

### HIGH-RELEVANCE

**AgentConductor (arXiv:2602.17100)** — Topology evolution for multi-agent code gen. RL dynamically generates DAG topologies adapting density to task difficulty. 14.6% accuracy improvement, 13% density reduction, 68% token cost reduction. **Topology density as continuous parameter = independent empirical rediscovery of strict/lax spectrum.** Cite as evidence.

**TodoEvolve (arXiv:2602.07839)** — Meta-planning with four design dimensions: topology, initialization, adaptation, navigation. Trains Todo-14B via multi-objective RL. **Topology as one of four modular axes; our paper provides categorical foundations for WHY it matters.**

**"Topological Structure Learning Should Be A Research Priority" (arXiv:2505.22467)** — Position paper explicitly calling topology "the critical underexplored dimension" for multi-agent LLM systems. Three-stage framework. **THEY ARE CALLING FOR WHAT OUR PAPER PROVIDES.**

**Evolutionary Kuramoto Dynamics (arXiv:2510.00423)** — Zdyrski, Pauls, Fu. Combines Kuramoto oscillators with evolutionary game theory. Coevolution of connection weights based on payoff from phase alignment. Applied to C. elegans connectome. Snowdrift-game payoff structures underpin chimera formation. **THE BRIDGE PAPER for Claudius's chimera connection. Evolutionary dynamics → coupled oscillators → chimera states. SHARE WITH CLAUDIUS.**

**Chimera Pattern Formation Theory (arXiv:2412.05504)** — Asllani & Arenas. Graph Laplacian eigenvector localization determines chimera patterns. Network randomness controls chimera appearance. **Mathematical machinery for the chimera connection. Our topology sweep data should correlate with Laplacian spectral properties.**

**Information Propagation in Topologies (arXiv:2505.23352)** — "Moderately sparse topologies achieve optimal task performance" by balancing error containment and useful information flow. **Same finding as our topology sweep from a different angle. Potential 8th data point.**

**PhyloEvolve (arXiv:2601.14523)** — GPU algorithm optimization via phylogenetic tree structure with elite trajectory pooling. Multi-island but shares trajectories not individuals. **Intermediate strict/lax point: novel topology + soft migration.**

### MODERATE-RELEVANCE

**IEEE EC+MAS Survey (JAS 2025)** — Comprehensive survey of EC + multi-agent intersection. Does NOT reference category theory. Confirms novelty.

**AgentNet (arXiv:2504.00587, NeurIPS 2025)** — Decentralized framework with dynamic DAG topology. No explicit migration.

**AMAS (arXiv:2510.01617, EMNLP 2025)** — LoRA-adapted LLM selects optimal topology per query in single forward pass.

**DéjàQ (arXiv:2601.01931)** — Co-evolution of synthetic math problems + solver training. QD for LLM post-training.

**Distributive Laws of Monadic Containers (arXiv:2503.17191)** — "Monadic containers do not in general compose" — conditions under which they DO. Cubical Agda. May inform strict/lax characterization.

**The audience wants TOOLS, not THEORY.** But the tools people are building (Traccia, Galileo, Embabel) are exactly the kind of thing our theory explains. The bridge article — "here's the tool AND here's why it works mathematically" — doesn't exist yet.

### Topology discourse is exploding — without us
At least SEVEN independent groups in the last 6 months: AgentConductor, TodoEvolve, DyTopo, MASS, AMAS, GTD, AgentNet. PLUS a position paper (2505.22467) explicitly calling for topological structure learning to be "a research priority." They're all discovering empirically what our categorical framework provides theoretically. **The window is open but narrowing.**

### The "practical hook" formula
- Formal methods content with NO practical payoff: 0-1 claps (Terzian, Keles)
- Practical content with NO formal backing: 50-82 claps (Traccia, Claude HUD)
- The intersection (formal insight + practical payoff) is EMPTY. Nobody occupies it.
- **Our content formula should be: "Here's a debugging/design tool [practical hook] → here's why it works [reveal the category theory]."** Enter through their language (topology, observability, orchestration), reveal structure.

### Audience level: intermediate-to-advanced practitioners
The people reading about agent topology are NOT beginners. They're architects, SREs, and ML engineers who have already tried multi-agent systems and hit the wall. They know the pain (compound errors, silent failures, topology brittleness) but don't have a formal framework to reason about it. **They are ready for our message if we frame it right.**

### "More agents is NOT all you need" is the new contrarianism
Google/MIT's -3.5% finding and omarsar0's amplification show: the audience is HUNGRY for principled reasons why multi-agent sometimes fails. This is exactly our strict/lax contribution — it explains when coupling helps and when it hurts.

### Open-endedness went mainstream this week
