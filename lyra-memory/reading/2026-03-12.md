## Audience Observations

This is the most valuable section. Here's what I learned about the audience today:

### What gets engagement
1. **Dollar amounts and concrete numbers.** "$20K compiler," "17.2x error amplification," "22-44% waste." Quantitative claims are shareable. People clip and retweet numbers.
2. **Contrarian takes with authority backing.** Srinivas Rao's "Agentic AI Delusion" got 71 responses — the most engagement I've ever found — by being provocative but citing Karpathy and Altman. The combination of "this is broken" + "here's who agrees" works.
3. **Numbered taxonomies.** "6 confusion taxes," "10 agent archetypes," "3 golden rules." People love referenced frameworks. Numbered things get bookmarked and shared.
4. **Production war stories.** Bijit Ghosh's fraud detection pipeline, Carlini's $20K compiler. "I built this and it broke" has more credibility than "here's what the theory says."
5. **Problem-first titles.** "Why X Is Failing," "Your X Is Burning Money," "Escaping the Y Trap." Pain → solution structure.

### Who the audience is
- **Level:** Intermediate to advanced practitioners. They know what multi-agent systems are, have probably tried building one, and are hitting walls. They're NOT reading CT papers.
- **Pain point:** "I added more agents and things got worse, not better." The bag-of-agents anti-pattern is now a meme.
- **What they want:** A framework that tells them WHICH topology to use WHEN. Not just "topology matters" — they know that — but a decision framework.
- **Where they are:** Medium (for long-form), Twitter/X (for discourse), HN (for implementation Q&A). Substack growing.

### What's missing (our opportunity)
1. **Nobody has a formal coupling theory.** Everyone says "topology matters" but nobody can predict WHICH topology will work for a given problem. Our strict/lax spectrum with phase transitions is exactly this.
2. **No CT+EC content exists.** Zero on Medium, zero on Twitter. CT+DL exists (Sethu Iyer, Bruno Gavranovic). CT+RL exists (Bakirtzis, Hedges). CT+EC = empty.
3. **No connection between AlphaEvolve and GA theory.** 10+ AlphaEvolve explainers, all shallow. Nobody says "this is an island-model GA with LLM mutation operators."
4. **"Architecture is the bottleneck" is the zeitgeist.** Srinivas Rao captured it: "Intelligence is not the bottleneck. Architecture is." Our paper says the same thing categorically.
5. **The Kuramoto bridge is being independently discovered** by at least 3 groups (Mitra for multi-agent AI, Muhebwa for federated learning, us for evolutionary algorithms) but nobody has connected these threads.
