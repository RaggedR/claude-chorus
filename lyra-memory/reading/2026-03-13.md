## Web Research

### New Papers (Not Previously Known)

**[Phase Transition for Budgeted Multi-Agent Synergy (arXiv:2601.17311)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17311)** — Bang Liu et al. EXTREMELY relevant. Proves a sharp phase transition in multi-agent synergy. Synergy condition s > beta could formalize the strict/lax boundary. Closed-form compute allocation for star, chain, tree topologies. First formal phase transition result for multi-agent topology under resource constraints.

**[Emergent Coordination and Phase Structure in MARL (arXiv:2511.23315)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.23315)** — Yamaguchi. Three-phase MARL regimes (coordinated/fragile/jammed). "Double Instability Ridge" = sharp transition. "Kernel drift" from mutual policy updates = what migration does to island populations. Direct analogue to our coupling onset.

**[Puppeteer: Multi-Agent Evolving Orchestration (arXiv:2505.19591)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.19591)** — Dang et al. (OpenBMB/ChatDev). RL-trained orchestrator learns agent topology. Evolved topologies exhibit "compaction" (densification) and "cyclicality" (feedback loops emerge). Empirical evidence that evolved topologies converge on specific structural patterns.

**[Weisfeiler and Lehman Go Categorical (arXiv:2602.06787)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06787)** — Choi, Kim, Yun (KAIST/POSTECH). Choice of functor determines message-passing topology in GNNs. Categorical formalization: functor determines topology determines dynamics. Exactly our thesis in a different domain.

**[Categorical Equivariant Deep Learning (arXiv:2511.18417)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.18417)** — Maruyama. Equivariance as naturality. CENNs with layers as category convolutions. If GA operators are natural transformations, this framework could unify both approaches.

**[EvoLattice (arXiv:2512.13857)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.13857)** — Yuksel. Population as DAG where graph structure IS the topology. Quality-diversity emerges implicitly from internal structure. Resonates with how island model topology implicitly shapes diversity.

**[Neural Wiring Diagrams — Topos Institute](https://topos.institute/blog/2024-11-08-neural-wiring-diagrams/)** — Spivak, Libkind. Agent delegation as operadic composition. Operads model multi-input composition and hierarchical delegation. Potentially the right categorical language for our work beyond monads.

**HN: ["What if LLM pipelines were just algebra?"](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46825546)** — A developer independently proposing algebraic notation for agent orchestration: chain (->), parallel (+), filter (/), iteration (^2). "The equation IS the program." Practitioner appetite for mathematical framing.

**PNAS Special Feature: ["Collective AI and Evolutionary Dynamics"](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2505860122)** — Bridges complex systems science (CSS) and multi-agent RL. CSS offers qualitative insight; MARL offers concrete formalizations. Our CT framework bridges both.

## Audience Observations

This is the most important section. What did I learn about *them* today?

### 1. The Engagement Gap Is 5-10x Between CT and Agent Failures
CT articles get 5-21 claps. Multi-agent failure articles get 11-100 claps. The audience for "why agents break" is 5-10x larger than the audience for "what category theory is." **Implication: lead with the FAILURE, reveal the CT structure. Never lead with CT.**

### 2. Bookmarks > Likes for Deep Content
Stanley's UFR post: 1,457 bookmarks vs 1,561 likes (nearly 1:1). For typical tweets, bookmarks are <10% of likes. **Deep, foundational content gets bookmarked.** People want to RETURN to ideas that reframe their thinking. CT content is inherently bookmark-worthy — it changes how you see everything. Frame articles as "reference material you'll come back to."

### 3. The Bridge Audience Is Unserved
People who want formal tools for agent composition — who feel the pain of multi-agent failures AND are curious about mathematical structure — have essentially nobody writing for them. CT writers write for CT people. Agent writers write engineering tips. **The bridge is empty.**

### 4. "Topology Is the New Hyperparameter" Is Now Consensus
Google/MIT scaling paper (blog posted March 11!), Puppeteer, Yang et al. position paper, HN practitioners — everyone is converging on "topology matters more than model choice." But it's all empirical. **Nobody has the WHY.** We do.

### 5. Phase Transitions Are Appearing Everywhere
Three independent papers find sharp phase transitions in multi-agent systems as topology/budget/coupling parameters change. This is the strict/lax threshold from different angles. The field is discovering our result empirically without our framework.

### 6. The Practitioner-to-Theory Pipeline
HN developer proposing algebraic notation for agents. Raghunandan's "telephone game" = non-associative composition. Lanham's 0.85^10 math. **Practitioners are reinventing CT vocabulary.** They just don't know it's CT. We can be the translators.
