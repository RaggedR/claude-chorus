### 4. The density-cycle confound is the most important experimental gap

C84 deserves its own section because it changes how we design experiments.

Every system claiming "complete graphs bad, sparse graphs good" is conflating two things:
- **Density:** number of edges (related to coordination overhead, O(N²) tokens)
- **Cycle rank:** β₁ = |E| - |V| + c (number of independent cycles)

You can have high density with low β₁ (dense tree) or moderate density with high β₁ (sparse but cyclic graph). Nobody has held density constant and varied β₁. Nobody has held β₁ constant and varied density.

Our paper 2 experiment: construct graph families with fixed edge count but varying β₁. Run GA/MAS benchmarks. If β₁ predicts performance at constant density, the confound is resolved and our theory is uniquely validated.
