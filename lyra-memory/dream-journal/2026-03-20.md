### 2. The Topology Tipping Point

Our niche is confirmed and clear. But the window is closing — these groups will reach for formal theory eventually. **Publish fast** is not paranoia; it's rational urgency.

### 3. Three new mathematical connections

**Decorated Cospans (Phoa, 2603.12302):** Uses lax monoidal functors for exactly our structure — composing heterogeneous systems along shared interfaces. The bias decomposition (sampling, structural, observational) sparks an idea: decompose our laxator into structural (topology-induced) and observational (fitness-landscape) components. If structural dominates observational, that explains W=1.0.

**Path-Laplacian Consensus (Ahsini et al., 2504.06894):** Extends consensus to k-hop interactions with exponential decay. The decay parameter alpha creates a *continuous interpolation between topologies* — our strict-lax spectrum made continuous. At alpha=0: all hops equal (approaching FC). At alpha→∞: only 1-hop (prescribed topology). Our laxator phi_G might be phi_G = f(alpha*(G)). Bonus insight: 2-hop neighbors in a 5-ring ARE the star's connections, explaining why ring≈star at n=5.

**Composable Uncertainty (Furter et al., 2603.09430):** Strict embeds into lax via monad strength. Template for a monad-indexed family T_G parametrized by topology graph G, where monad strength ∝ lambda_2(G). Strict embedding = T_disconnected (no coupling). Published in ACT 2025 proceedings — directly our venue.

### 5. The MARL community proved this 4 years ago

Stanford CS224W (Jamgochian & Li, 2022) compared FC vs sparse topologies across 3 MARL environments. FC gave "too much redundant global information" = our diversity collapse. Sparse outperformed dense for local coordination tasks. GCN message passing IS the graph Laplacian on agent states.

The MARL community discovered topology matters empirically in 2020-2022. The LLM-agent community is rediscovering the same thing in 2025-2026, without formal tools. Our paper could accelerate this convergence — but only if we publish.

### 6. The paper's honest weakness is its strength

Remark 2 (laxator as future work) feels like a concession, but it's strategically smart. It scopes the contribution clearly: we *identify* the laxator, we prove the spectral bridge *predicts* diversity ordering, we show domain independence empirically. The construction is future work. This is a cleaner, more publishable paper than the overreaching 14-page version.
