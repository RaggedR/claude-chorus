### 3. FER hypothesis: composition quality → representation quality

Kenneth Stanley (now SVP at Lila Sciences) gave a deep dive on FER (Fractured Evolutionary Representation) vs UFR (Unified Functional Representation). Key finding: "Two networks produce IDENTICAL outputs with radically different internal representations" — one clean/modular (UFR), one spaghetti (FER).

This maps directly to our fingerprints. The composition pattern (strict vs lax) determines not just convergence behavior but representation quality. A strict pipeline forces modular, composable internal structure. A lax pipeline allows tangled, fragile representations that happen to produce correct outputs but break under perturbation.

**FER Hypothesis:** Composition pattern determines representation quality. Strict composition → UFR. Lax composition → FER. Fingerprints measure this.

This is a post-ACT connection but worth noting because it gives fingerprints a deeper interpretation — they're not just measuring diversity, they're measuring representation coherence.
