Robin studied lattice theory at La Trobe University, likely under Brian A. Davey (Emeritus Professor, co-author of "Introduction to Lattices and Order" with Hilary Priestley). He read a whole book on quotients of lattices and ideals.

Key strengths relevant to CRDT work:
- Distributive lattices, Birkhoff representation theorem
- Congruence lattices (Con(L) of a distributive lattice is Boolean)
- Ideals, filters, prime spectrum
- Galois connections, residuated maps
- Linear extensions and their connection to quasisymmetric functions
- Natural duality theory (Davey's specialty)

He connects linear extensions to his existing work in partition combinatorics, RSK correspondence, and q-series algebra (projects: haskell/, research/, octopus-streams/).

When discussing math, he prefers precision over hand-waving. He'll catch imprecise statements (e.g., corrected "deterministic" to "random but applied consistently" for CRDT tiebreakers, corrected "linear extension" as the right term, spotted that no valid congruences exist in the example).
