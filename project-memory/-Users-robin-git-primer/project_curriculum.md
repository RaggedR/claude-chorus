**Full curriculum** (7 subjects): history, persuasive writing, Chinese, mathematics, Haskell, drawing, music

**Haskell chosen over Python** as the primary programming language. Rationale: Haskell forces mathematical thinking, pattern matching, and recursion. It teaches imagination and structural reasoning rather than vocational skills. Python may still be used for experimental mathematics (plotting, simulation).

**Mathematics is combinatorics-first, no calculus.** Rationale: combinatorics is discrete, concrete, visual, connects directly to Haskell and to proof. Calculus can be learned later if needed for engineering. The mechanical parts of calculus are exactly what AI does well — not worth teaching. The ideas of calculus (rates of change, accumulation) can be encountered through generating functions and finite differences.

**Yang Hui's triangle (楊輝三角)** — Pascal's triangle 300 years earlier. Natural cultural bridge between the Chinese and mathematics curricula.

**Why:** Robin believes calculus should be completely removed from the school curriculum. AI handles symbolic manipulation; what remains worth teaching is reasoning, proof, and the ability to explore.

**How to apply:** When designing math content, lean combinatorial. Proof is the thread — every level involves arguing that an answer is correct, connecting to the persuasive writing gates.
